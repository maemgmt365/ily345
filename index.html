the story

this didn’t start as a brand.

it started from something real.

someone once shared the idea of 345 with me through something simple: the more things you say yes to, the more things you have to say no to.

when you’re building something, chasing something, or just locked in on what you want, sometimes the people you care about get less of you. not because you don’t care — just because you’re focused.

for me, that showed up in missed conversations, short responses, and not always being present the way I wanted to be.

so instead of leaving it there, I’d just send 345 — a simple way of saying I’m here, I’m thinking about you, and I still mean these words.

over time, this became something deeper for me.

it was how my mom and i stayed connected — a simple way to say what mattered, even when conversations weren’t perfect.

after she passed, those words carried more weight.

it made me more intentional — about not leaving things unsaid.

whether in absence or in abundance… these are words we don’t hear enough.

a sign-off.
a check-in.
a habit.
